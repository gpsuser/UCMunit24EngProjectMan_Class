### Question 1

**Source:** Question paper — January 2023 (Unit 24), Question 4(b)(i) | **[4 marks]**

**Case study context:**

Better Health plc is a UK-based health supplements company. The company has recently completed a project to develop and launch a new range of nutritional supplements aimed at sports and fitness customers. Sarah is the Project Manager for the project. Now that the project has closed, a review is being conducted, and end-user feedback has been collected from customers.

**Question:**

Explain the benefits to Better Health plc of identifying project strengths and lessons learned from Sarah's project.

*(4 marks)*

---

### Question 2

**Source:** Question paper — January 2024 (Unit 24), Question 4(b)(iii) | **[2 marks]**

**Case study context:**

HD Tyre Ltd is a UK-based tyre manufacturer. The company has just completed a project to implement carbon reduction measures across three of its manufacturing sites. Ben is the project sponsor. Charlie is the project manager. A final project report has been produced, which documents outcomes, variance data, and stakeholder feedback.

**Question:**

Explain one way Ben could use the final project report to drive business improvement.

*(2 marks)*

### Question 3

**Adapted from:** January 2023 (Unit 24), Question 4(b)(i) | **[4 marks]**

**Case study context:**

Merefield Utilities Ltd has recently completed a project to install smart metering systems across 2,000 residential properties on behalf of a regional energy supplier. Priya is the Project Manager. The project was delivered on time but finished 8% over the approved budget. A post-project review has been completed and feedback has been collected from the energy supplier, installation teams, and householders.

**Question:**

Explain the benefits to Merefield Utilities Ltd of using end-of-project feedback to recognise the successes and failures of Priya's project.

*(4 marks)*

---

### Question 4

**Adapted from:** June 2024 (Unit 24), Question 4(c)(ii) and January 2024 (Unit 24), Question 4(b)(iii) | **[4 marks]**

**Case study context:**

Norbridge Construction Ltd has just completed a bridge refurbishment project for a local council. The project was Norbridge's first major infrastructure contract. The project manager, Jamie, has produced a detailed final report which includes a lessons-learned register. Several issues arose during the project, including unexpected groundwater on-site, a delay in receiving specialist steelwork, and a miscommunication between the design team and the site crew. Norbridge has not previously conducted formal lessons-learned reviews.

**Question:**

Explain two ways in which Norbridge Construction Ltd could use the lessons learned from Jamie's project to improve the company's future performance.

*(4 marks — 2 marks per way)*

### Question 5

**[4 marks]**

**Case study context:**

Kelvin Power Ltd has completed a project to upgrade turbine control systems at a gas-fired power station. The project overran by four months due to two key issues: a failure by a component supplier to deliver critical parts on time, and a software integration error that required significant rework. The project team is conducting a lessons-learned workshop.

**Question:**

Using the **5 Whys** technique, explain how the project team at Kelvin Power Ltd could begin to identify the root cause of the software integration error. Explain why this process is more useful than simply recording that the error occurred.

*(4 marks)*

---

### Question 6

**[4 marks]**

**Case study context:**

The project sponsor at Kelvin Power Ltd wants to understand the level of satisfaction among two key groups following the completion of the turbine upgrade project: (i) the main client, National Grid Electricity Transmission (NGET), and (ii) members of the project team.

**Question:**

Describe **two indicators** that the project manager at Kelvin Power Ltd could use to monitor and measure stakeholder satisfaction — one indicator for each of the following stakeholder groups:

(i)  The client (NGET)
(ii) Members of the project team

*(4 marks — 2 marks per indicator)*

---

### Question 7

**[12 marks]**

**Case study context:**

Anders Engineering plc has recently completed a two-year project to retrofit its main manufacturing facility in Sheffield with energy-efficient HVAC (heating, ventilation and air conditioning) systems. The project was completed on time but finished 11% over the approved budget. Several subcontractors performed below expectations, producing work that had to be partially redone, and the standard of written documentation produced throughout the project was poor. The project sponsor, Diane, is preparing a final report for the company board.

**Question:**

Evaluate the different ways in which Diane and Anders Engineering plc could use feedback from this project to improve the organisation's performance. In your answer, justify which use of feedback would be of greatest benefit to Anders Engineering plc.

*(12 marks)*

> **Examiner's guidance:** A strong response will identify several distinct uses of feedback (LO6.3), develop each with a clear explanation, apply each use specifically to the Anders Engineering plc scenario, and make a justified judgement about which use is most valuable. Vague or generic answers that are not linked to the scenario will not achieve the higher mark bands.
