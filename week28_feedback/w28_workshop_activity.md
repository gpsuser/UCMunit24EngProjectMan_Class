### Question 5

An engineering company has recently completed a large civil infrastructure project. The project manager, Danielle, is preparing her end-of-project review. She has access to the following information:

- The final construction cost compared with the approved project budget
- An interview with the client's chief engineer, who shares her views on the quality of workmanship
- A record of all issues logged during the project, showing when each was raised and resolved

**(a)** For each piece of information listed above, state whether it represents **objective** or **subjective** feedback. Justify each answer in one sentence.

*(6 marks — 2 marks per item: 1 for correct classification, 1 for justification)*

**(b)** Explain **one reason** why Danielle should gather **both** objective and subjective feedback when measuring the success of the project, rather than relying on one type alone.

*(2 marks)*

### Question 7

Read the following case study and answer the questions that follow.

> **Case Study: Thames Tideway Tunnel**
>
> The Thames Tideway Tunnel is a major civil engineering project designed to upgrade London's Victorian sewerage system. At project completion, the project sponsor wishes to evaluate whether the project has met its aims and objectives. The project team has the following feedback available:
>
> - Completion figures showing the actual tunnel length constructed versus the planned specification
> - Responses from a post-project satisfaction survey distributed to 200 stakeholders
> - Informal conversations with local community representatives about their experience of the construction period
> - A record of the total number of formal complaints received from residents, categorised by issue type

**(a)** From the list above, identify **one example of objective feedback** and **one example of subjective feedback**. For each, give a reason why it fits that category.

*(4 marks — 2 marks per feedback type)*

**(b)** The project sponsor is considering whether to use the satisfaction survey results as the **sole method** of measuring project success.

Evaluate whether this is an appropriate approach. In your answer, refer to the features, benefits, and limitations of satisfaction surveys.

*(4 marks)*
