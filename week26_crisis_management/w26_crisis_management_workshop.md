### Instructor Note — Question Selection Rationale

Week 26 (Crisis Management) has no directly mapped past OCR examination questions in any series from 2022 to 2024. This is by design: Week 26 is a workshop session that applies and consolidates the monitoring and factors content from Weeks 23–25. Accordingly, the two questions in **Section A** have been drawn from the immediately adjacent LO5 delivery weeks:

- **Section A, Question 1** is taken from the January 2023 examination paper, Question 4(a)(iii), which aligns with Week 23 (Monitoring Methods, LO5.1). Resource shortages causing time creep directly underpins the concept of resource crises introduced in Week 26.
- **Section A, Question 2** is taken from the June 2023 examination paper, Question 3(d), which aligns with Weeks 24–25 (Internal and External Factors, LO5.2). The ability to distinguish internal from external crisis origins is a core skill in Week 26.

Questions from the 2025 examination series have been excluded throughout, in accordance with workshop preparation guidelines.

### Question 3

**Alignment:** Weeks 23/26 — Monitoring Methods; Crisis Classification | LO5.1

**Context:** Bridgeways Engineering Ltd is managing a major river crossing project in the North of England. The project involves the construction of a new cable-stayed road bridge. The project manager, Priya, has recently received a number of issue reports from site supervisors and must classify each one by its level of severity in order to determine the appropriate response.

The following severity levels are defined for this project:

```
+----------------+----------------------------------------------------+
| Severity Level | Definition                                         |
+----------------+----------------------------------------------------+
| Critical       | Will prevent project completion unless resolved    |
|                | within 24 hours                                    |
+----------------+----------------------------------------------------+
| High           | Will breach a major project constraint (time,      |
|                | cost, or quality) if unresolved within 1 week      |
+----------------+----------------------------------------------------+
| Medium         | Will affect the project if not resolved within     |
|                | 1 month                                            |
+----------------+----------------------------------------------------+
| Low            | Unlikely to affect the project significantly       |
+----------------+----------------------------------------------------+

```
+---+---------------------------------------------------------------+
| # | Issue Description                                             |

### Question 6

**New question | Week 26 — Crisis Management | LO5.1**

During the construction of a new railway station on a regional rail network, the project manager discovers that the structural steelwork for the station canopy is running six weeks behind schedule. The activity is on the critical path. The project sponsor has made clear that the contractual completion date cannot be extended.

The project manager is considering two corrective measures:

- **Option 1:** Fast-tracking — overlapping the canopy steelwork installation with the fit-out of the station concourse, which was originally planned to begin only after all steelwork was complete.
- **Option 2:** Crashing — recruiting an additional specialist steelwork contractor to work in parallel with the existing team, at an estimated additional cost of £85,000 per week.

**Analyse** the advantages and disadvantages of **each** option for this project, and **recommend** which corrective measure the project manager should implement, justifying your recommendation.

*[6 marks]*

### Question 7

**New question | Week 26 — Crisis Management | LO5.1, LO5.2**

A project to design and manufacture a new lightweight composite fuselage section for a regional aircraft has experienced a significant crisis. A structural defect was discovered during prototype testing, requiring extensive redesign of two sub-assemblies. The project is now estimated to be 14 weeks behind the original delivery date and 22% over budget.

The project sponsor has asked the project manager to produce a recovery plan.

**Evaluate** the key components that a robust recovery plan should contain in this situation, explaining the purpose of each component and why stakeholder agreement is essential to the success of the recovery plan.
