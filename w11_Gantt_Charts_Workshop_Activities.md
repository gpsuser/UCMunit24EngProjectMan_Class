## Helpful Hints for Learners

### When completing Gantt charts:
1. Always read the dependency column carefully
2. Start with activities that have no dependencies (they begin at time 0)
3. Each subsequent activity begins when its predecessor(s) finish
4. Draw bars accurately to scale using the timeline
5. Check your final chart: does the last activity finish at your calculated minimum time?

### When calculating minimum completion time:
1. Identify all possible paths from start to finish
2. Calculate the duration of each path by adding activity durations
3. The longest path is the critical path
4. The duration of the critical path is the minimum completion time

### When explaining advantages/disadvantages:
1. Make specific reference to project management benefits
2. Use appropriate technical terminology (e.g., critical path, resource allocation, schedule compression)
3. Link your explanation to consequences for time, cost, or quality
4. Give practical examples where appropriate

### Understanding slack time:
- Slack time = Latest Finish Time - Earliest Finish Time
- Critical activities have zero slack time
- Non-critical activities can be delayed by their slack time without affecting project completion
- Slack time provides scheduling flexibility for resource management

## Notes for Lecturers

**Workshop Management:**

1. **Introduction (2 minutes)**: Briefly explain the three-section structure and time allocations

2. **Working time (25 minutes)**: 
   - Encourage learners to attempt Section 1 first (past paper questions) as these provide the clearest assessment of fundamental understanding
   - Circulate to provide guidance, but avoid giving direct answers
   - For learners who finish early, encourage them to check their work or attempt extension questions

3. **Wrap-up (3 minutes)**: 
   - Highlight common issues observed
   - Preview that solutions will be provided separately
   - Remind learners that these question styles will appear in their examination

**Differentiation:**

- **Support**: Provide pre-drawn Gantt chart grids for Questions 3 and 5
- **Extension**: Ask advanced learners to calculate Latest Finish Times and identify total float vs free float
- **Alternative**: Learners struggling with written explanations could verbally explain to the lecturer

**Common Errors to Watch For:**

1. **Question 1 & 3**: Starting dependent activities at the wrong time; incorrect bar lengths
2. **Question 2 & 4**: Vague explanations lacking project management terminology
3. **Question 5**: Adding all durations instead of finding the critical path
4. **Question 6**: Misunderstanding slack time calculation
5. **Question 7**: Not distinguishing between activities with slack vs critical activities

- Gantt chart construction accuracy
