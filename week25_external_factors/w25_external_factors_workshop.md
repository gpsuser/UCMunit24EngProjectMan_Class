### Question 2

*(Source: June 2023 Examination Paper, Question 4(c))*

Sykes Components Ltd, a British manufacturer, undertook a project to purchase a specialist industrial robot from The Kaashawi Corporation, a Japanese supplier, at a budgeted cost of £150,000. Upon completing the project, the finance team identified an adverse variance: the actual cost of purchasing the robot exceeded the original budget.

Identify **TWO** economic factors that may have caused the adverse variance in the robot purchase budget. For each factor, explain how it could have contributed to the cost increase.

**(6 marks)**

### Question 3

*(Adapted from: January 2023 Examination Paper, Question 2(d))*

*Original question: 2 marks. This version is expanded to 4 marks.*

A project team at an engineering firm is developing a new compact electric motor for use in light commercial vehicles. Midway through the project, the project manager becomes aware that two competitor firms are also developing similar products and are expected to bring them to market within the next year.

### Question 7

A project manager at a UK precision engineering company is leading a project to develop a new CNC machining cell to increase production capacity. The project is currently on schedule and within budget. However, the project manager has just read a trade publication reporting that steel prices have risen significantly due to global supply shortages, and that the government is consulting on new environmental regulations that would require manufacturers to report embodied carbon in their capital equipment purchases.

Analyse the likely impact of these two external factors on the project. In your answer, you should:

- identify which PESTLE categories each factor belongs to
- explain the specific effect each factor could have on project outcomes
- recommend one monitoring action the project manager should take in response to each factor
