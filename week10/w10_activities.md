## Helpful Hints

### General Guidance

1. **Read questions carefully**: Identify the command word (define, explain, evaluate) and respond accordingly
2. **Use the scenario**: Where questions reference the railway project, apply your knowledge to this specific context
3. **Show your thinking**: For longer questions, structure answers with clear points
4. **Technical terminology**: Use correct project management terminology from the unit specification
5. **Examples strengthen answers**: Where appropriate, reference aspects of the railway scenario
6. **Mark allocation guides depth**: A 2-mark question needs less detail than an 8-mark question

### Command Word Guidance

- **Define** (1-2 marks): Provide a clear, precise meaning
- **State/Identify** (1 mark each): Brief, factual responses
- **Explain** (2-4 marks): Give reasons, causes or how something works
- **Evaluate** (5-8 marks): Weigh evidence, consider multiple perspectives, make judgements

### Specific Question Hints

**Question 1**: Think about the very beginning of a project - what needs to be established?

**Question 2**: Communication strategies cover who needs information, what information, when and how.

**Question 3**: Consider physical items, technology and people as distinct resource categories.

**Question 4**: Power refers to decision-making authority; interest refers to concern about project outcomes.

**Question 5**: Think about what a project manager does on a daily basis to keep the project moving.

**Question 6**: Consider whether to avoid, reduce or manage conflict, and specific techniques.

**Question 7**: Remember Hersey and Blanchard's situational leadership model (directive, supportive, collaborative, responsive).

**Question 8**: Consider impacts on schedule, costs, team morale, stakeholder confidence and project success.

## Glossary of Key Terms

**ETCS (European Train Control System)**: Digital train protection system that monitors and controls train speed

**Signalling**: Railway control systems that manage train movements and ensure safe separation between trains

**Control Centre**: Central facility where signalling and train movements are monitored and controlled

**Train-borne Equipment**: Onboard systems installed on trains to interface with trackside signalling

**Rail Regulator**: Government agency responsible for railway safety standards and compliance

**Line Capacity**: Maximum number of trains that can safely operate on a section of track in a given time period

**Project Lifecycle**: Complete sequence of stages from project initiation through to closure

**Stakeholder**: Any individual, group or organisation with interest in or influence over project outcomes

**Power-Interest Matrix**: Tool for classifying stakeholders based on their authority and concern levels

**Contingency**: Additional time or budget allocated to address potential risks and uncertainties
