### Question 3

**Adapted from: Question paper January 2022 (Unit 24) — Questions 2(a) and 3(b)**

**Case study context:**

*Meridian Rail Engineering Ltd has been commissioned to upgrade the signalling and communications systems along a 40 km stretch of mainline railway. The project involves replacing legacy control hardware, installing new software platforms, and integrating the updated system with the national rail network's digital infrastructure. Imogen, the project manager, is assessing the technological resources the project will require.*

**(a)** Explain what is meant by **computer hardware** as a technological resource, giving **one example** relevant to the Meridian Rail Engineering project.

**(2 marks)**

**(b)** Explain what is meant by **software** as a technological resource, giving **one example** relevant to the Meridian Rail Engineering project.

**(2 marks)**

### Question 4

**Adapted from: Question paper June 2024 (Unit 24) — Questions 3(d)(i) and 3(d)(ii)**

**Case study context:**

*Apex Structures Ltd is managing the installation of a new automated testing rig at its manufacturing facility. The project team requires access to a specialist CNC machine to carry out precision machining tests that form part of the installation process. However, the company's only CNC machine is currently committed to production orders for the next three weeks, and the project cannot proceed as scheduled. The project manager, Dev, is considering hiring an external precision machining firm to complete the tests at their premises instead.*

**(a)** Identify the category of **resource constraint** that the unavailability of the CNC machine represents.

**(1 mark)**

**(b)** Explain **one benefit** and **one drawback** to the Apex Structures project of Dev's decision to hire an external precision machining firm.

**(4 marks — 2 × 2 marks)**

### Question 6

**New question — poor leadership**

**Case study context:**

*Emma is the project manager for a civil engineering firm overseeing the refurbishment of a road bridge. Over the past month, Emma has not held any progress meetings with her project team. She has not updated the project schedule for six weeks. She has also failed to respond to concerns raised by two site engineers regarding a delay in the delivery of structural reinforcement steel.*

Explain **two** likely consequences to the bridge refurbishment project of Emma's **poor leadership**.

### Question 7

**New question — ethics and CSR as an internal factor**

A project manager at a UK energy company is overseeing the installation of a new offshore wind turbine array. The company has a corporate social responsibility (CSR) policy requiring all projects to source at least 60% of materials from UK-based suppliers.

**(a)** Explain **one** way in which this CSR policy could act as an **internal constraint** on the project.

**(2 marks)**

**(b)** Explain **one** potential benefit to the project or the company of maintaining this policy.

**(2 marks)**
